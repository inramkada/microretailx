PREAMBLE AND STATEMENT OF PURPOSE

This legal instrument constitutes a comprehensive regulatory framework governing access, utilization, and commercial exploitation of the technological platform denominated XSHADOWS OPS, developed and operated by MICRORETAILX LLC (hereinafter, "the Company").

WHEREAS the transnational nature of digital operations demands normative design ensuring legal certainty across multiple jurisdictions;

WHEREAS the incorporation of artificial intelligence systems presents unprecedented regulatory challenges requiring anticipatory governance frameworks;

WHEREAS personal data protection constitutes a fundamental right recognized under Article 8 of the Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union and equivalent constitutional provisions;

WHEREAS the Company seeks to establish industry-leading standards for technological compliance, algorithmic transparency, and cross-border data governance;

The Company hereby establishes this contractual regime under the principles of transparency, proportionality, and proactive accountability.

Statement of Legal Architecture

This document has been structured in accordance with the legal drafting standards typically applied in complex international digital infrastructure projects. Its composition reflects an anticipatory legal architecture tailored to environments characterized by regulatory volatility, transnational exposure, and technological opacity.

Multijurisdictional Alignment

The Terms integrate a coherent compliance framework encompassing the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and internationally recognized ethical principles for artificial intelligence, including the OECD AI Principles and the UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI. This synthesis is designed to ensure operational resilience across both civil law and common law jurisdictions.

Strategic Jurisdictional Anchoring

Dispute resolution mechanisms are anchored in the Courts of Barcelona and the State of Delaware, reflecting a deliberate selection of jurisdictions renowned for legal certainty and enforceability in commercial and technology-driven disputes. The governing law provisions are drafted to mitigate forum shopping and procedural inefficiencies.

AI Governance and Liability Shielding

A clear distinction is established between AI-generated outputs and human decision-making responsibilities. The allocation of liability in relation to algorithmic interpretation is mapped via a dedicated Responsibility Matrix, serving as an internal risk mitigation tool and an external demonstration of due diligence.

This legal construct is intended not merely to ensure compliance, but to function as a defensive legal perimeter—capable of withstanding litigation, regulatory inspection, and investor due diligence across multiple jurisdictions and operational regimes.

TITLE IGENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1. Definitions and Legal Terminology

For the purposes of this instrument, the following definitions shall have normative value:

  • "Platform" or "System": Comprehensive technological infrastructure comprising proprietary software architecture, artificial intelligence modules, APIs, database systems, cloud computing services, and real-time analytics engines.
  • "Qualified User": Natural person of legal age with full juridical capacity, or legal entity duly incorporated under its national law, accessing the System for commercial, professional, or enterprise purposes.
  • "Data Processing": As defined in Article 4(2) of Regulation (EU) 2016/679, any operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, or destruction.
  • "Algorithmic Output": Any recommendation, prediction, or decision-support information generated through machine learning models or AI technologies. Such outputs are inherently probabilistic and shall not be construed as deterministic instructions.
  • "Cross-Border Data Transfer": Any transmission of personal data from the European Economic Area to third countries, subject to Chapter V GDPR adequacy requirements.
  • "Economic Substance": The genuine business activities, decision-making processes, and value-creating functions undertaken by the Company in its jurisdiction of incorporation, evidenced by personnel, assets, premises, and operational control.
  • "Permanent Establishment": A fixed place of business through which enterprise business is carried on, as defined under applicable double taxation treaties and domestic tax legislation.
  • "Digital Services": Electronic services provided via internet or electronic networks, subject to jurisdiction-specific VAT/GST regimes under EU Directive 2008/8/EC and equivalent legislation.
  • "Force Majeure": Any extraordinary circumstance beyond reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, armed conflicts, governmental regulations, cyberattacks exceeding industry-standard defenses, or pandemic declarations by the WHO.

Article 9. Liability Limitation and Indemnification

9.1 Limitation of Liability

Company's aggregate liability is limited to:

  • Direct damages: Lesser of €500,000 or 12 months of fees paid
  • Indirect damages: Excluded except for willful misconduct
  • Data breach: Statutory minimums under applicable data protection law
  • IP infringement: Full indemnification with reasonable defense costs

9.2 Mutual Indemnification

Each party indemnifies the other against third-party claims arising from:

  • Breach of representations and warranties
  • Violation of applicable laws or regulations
  • Negligent or willful misconduct
  • Misuse of confidential information

9.3 Force Majeure

Neither party bears liability for delays or failures due to force majeure events, provided:

  • Prompt notification to the other party
  • Reasonable efforts to mitigate impact
  • Alternative performance where commercially feasible
  • Termination right if event exceeds 180 days
